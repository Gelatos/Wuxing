Creation

▣ ▢ Build (RSN)
Build is the skill to create structures and objects. This skill includes several different forms of artistic impression such as through drawing, sculpting, handcrafting of fine objects, and conveying art and information through images and technique.
▾ ▸
▣ ▢ Channel (RSN)
Channel is the skill to maintain concentration on ether you have manipulated in order to continue its effects. Magical effects that create a sustained area of effect typically use channel to determine their effectiveness.
▾ ▸
▣ ▢ Concoct (INT)
Concoct is the skill of combining ingredients inorder to create a new product. If you are cooking a meal, making medicine, or otherwise blending items together to form a new item you would use the mix skill.
▾ ▸
▣ ▢ Enchant (CNV)
Enchant is the skill to control ones own ether and impart it into another person or object. This is the basis for techniques like healing and empowerment magic.
Sensing
▾ ▸
▣ ▢ Analyze (RSN)
When attempting to find clues and make deductions based on those clues, you make an Analyze check. You might deduce the location of a hidden object, discern from the appearance of a wound what kind of weapon dealt it, or determine the weakest point in a tunnel that could cause it to collapse. Poring through books in search of a hidden fragment of knowledge might also call for an Analyze check.
▾ ▸
▣ ▢ Empathy (INT)
Empathy is used to sense both feelings in other creatures and any existing mana or ether in the environment.
▾ ▸
▣ ▢ Resonance (CNV)
Resonance is the ability to detect and release ether. It is most often used to detect magical structures and as communication with spirits. Less commonly, it can be used to destroy magical effects and structures made from ether that hasn't been permanently bound.
▾ ▸
▣ ▢ Search (INT)
This is used to actively search for for clues or anything out of sorts. You might deduce the location of a hidden object, discern from the appearance of a wound what kind of weapon dealt it, or determine the weakest point in a tunnel that could cause it to collapse.
▾ ▸
▣ ▢ Survival (BOD)
Survival is the ability to stay alive in ex- treme environmental conditions for extended periods of time. The skill governs a character’s ability to per- form vital outdoor tasks such as start a fire, build a shel- ter, scrounge for food, etc.
Social
▾ ▸
▣ ▢ Charm (INT)
Enticing, fascinating, and endearing others to you on a personal basis. It can be used to win someone over emotionally through friendliness, joy, and an ability to read a situation.
▾ ▸
▣ ▢ Command (CNV)
Command is the ability to use perceived authority or intimidation to control another's perceptions and action. One may use command to inspire a companion, intimidate a foe into backing down, or when making demands of another.
▾ ▸
▣ ▢ Deception (CNV)
Deception is the skill of telling convincing lies, as well as feigning emotion, belief, or frame of mind. Deception can encompass everything from misleading others through ambiguity to telling outright lies. This skill is also used to convince others of a disguise.
▾ ▸
▣ ▢ Negotiation (RSN)
Negotiation governs a character’s ability to apply their charisma, tactics, and knowledge of situational psychology in order to create a better position when making deals.
Item
▾ ▸
▣ ▢ Finesse (QCK)
Finesse is used to strike with light and nimble weapons such as daggers, shortswords, and rapiers. Those that use finesse weapons will often fight deftly, exploiting weaknesses in an enemy's defenses. Weapons in this group are typically easy to hide and allow their wielders to exploit a brace vulnerability. Techniques that support this skill will often grant more mobility options to a character.
▾ ▸
▣ ▢ Might (BOD)
This skill allows one to attack with brute strength while wielding heavy and cumbersome weapons. These weapons support large damage values allowing their wielders to smash through their enemies. Weapons in this group often will pierce through armor or exploit a reflex vulnerability.
▾ ▸
▣ ▢ Pilot (QCK)
When attempting to drive a vehicle of any kind, the pilot skill often governs most checks.
▾ ▸
▣ ▢ Shoot (PRC)
The skill of using a bow or firearm. These weapons have the most variety in weapon ranges allowing one to reliably attack from a distance.
▾ ▸
▣ ▢ Skirmish (PRC)
This skill governs most balanced, close range fighting styles such as with longswords, clubs, and polearms. Weapons in this category offer the most variety of technique options to manipulate the battlefield to their favor. Unique to this group are weapons that can exploit both reflex and brace vulnerabilities.
▾ ▸
▣ ▢ Throw (PRC)
When one strikes at a foe or aims for a location by throwing an object, it is typical to use the throw skill. Many melee weapons will have a range increment. In order to use this range the user will instead use the Throw skill to determine accuracy.
▾ ▸
▣ ▢ Tinker (PRC)
This skill covers building, repairing, and disabling mechanical devices such as locks, tools, and machinery.
